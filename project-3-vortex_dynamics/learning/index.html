    LEARNINGS

    1. Flow Instability is Driven by Shear

    • Velocity difference between layers creates instability
    • Even small disturbances can grow rapidly

    Shear is the main reason for vortex formation

    2. Small Perturbations Have Big Effects

    • A tiny sinusoidal disturbance leads to large vortices
    • Growth is exponential in the early stage

    Fluid systems are highly sensitive to initial conditions

    3. Transition from Linear to Nonlinear Flow

    • Initial stage → smooth wave growth
    • Later stage → vortex roll-up

    This shows how flow evolves from simple to complex

    4. Importance of Vorticity

    • Vorticity clearly highlights vortex structures
    • Better than velocity magnitude for visualization

    Vorticity is key to understanding rotational flow

    5. Effect of Viscosity

    • Low viscosity → sharp, well-defined vortices
    • High viscosity → diffused structures

    Viscosity controls how “clean” the flow looks

    6. Grid Resolution Matters

    • Higher resolution captures finer details
    • Lower resolution introduces numerical diffusion

    Accuracy depends strongly on grid quality

    7. Numerical vs Physical Diffusion

    • Physical diffusion → due to viscosity
    • Numerical diffusion → due to discretization

    Both affect the final result

    8. Understanding Flow Physics

    This project helped build intuition about:

    • Instability growth
    • Vortex formation
    • Flow evolution

    This is fundamental to turbulence and real-world CFD

    Final Takeaway

    A simple shear flow can evolve into complex vortex structures due to instability mechanisms.
