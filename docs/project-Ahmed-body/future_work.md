# Future Work

Although the current simulation successfully captures the overall flow behavior, several improvements can be made to enhance accuracy and reliability.

---

## 1. Mesh Refinement

- Increase mesh resolution in wake region  
- Add local refinement near the rear slant  
- Improve boundary layer resolution  

👉 Expected Impact:
Better prediction of separation and wake structure, leading to more accurate drag values

---

## 2. Grid Independence Study

- Run simulations with multiple mesh densities  
- Compare drag coefficient across meshes  

👉 Expected Impact:
Ensure results are not dependent on mesh size

---

## 3. Near-Wall Modeling

- Refine prism layers near walls  
- Maintain appropriate y+ values  

👉 Expected Impact:
Improved boundary layer prediction and separation accuracy

---

## 4. Time Averaging

- Compute time-averaged drag coefficient  
- Analyze fluctuations in drag force  

👉 Expected Impact:
More realistic representation of transient flow behavior

---

## 5. Validation with Literature

- Compare results with published Ahmed body studies  
- Benchmark against experimental data  

👉 Expected Impact:
Increase confidence in simulation results

---

## 6. Improved Domain Size

- Extend domain boundaries if required  
- Reduce blockage effects  

👉 Expected Impact:
More realistic external flow conditions

---

## 7. Advanced Turbulence Modeling

- Explore DES or LES approaches  

👉 Expected Impact:
Better capture of unsteady wake structures

---

## Final Direction

The next step is to transition from:

- Qualitative understanding  
→ to  
- Quantitative accuracy

by improving mesh quality, validation, and modeling fidelity.
